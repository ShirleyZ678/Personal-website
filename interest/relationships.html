Relatioinsip is typically between: friends, family, and romantic partner. A series of interactions with the same individual does not constitute a relationship We defined psychologically in terms of: Distress and longing in separation (grief being an extreme example) Sense of well-being in their presence Relationship is also known as ‘social bonds’: Attachment being a particularly intense type of social bond, normally reserved for close family and long-term romantic partners.

Why Relationship matters? Affiliation is associated with positive affect, and absence of affiliation is a powerful motivator to connect with others. Benefits for health: Social support protects against cardiovascular, immune, and endocrine disease (Uchino et al., 1996) Loneliness associated with greater cognitive decline in the elderly (Wilson et al., 2007) Benefits over evolutionary time: Promotes group cohesiveness (over pure self interest); nurturing of the young, etc.; all of which have survival benefits
